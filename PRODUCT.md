# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indie founders and solo operators running B2B SaaS businesses on Stripe. Primary user opens the dashboard when something feels wrong with revenue — a spike in churn, a failed payment notice, a gut feeling. They are time-poor and emotionally invested in the numbers. Secondary use: a quick weekly pulse check. They are not data analysts; they want to know "am I okay?" in under ten seconds.

## Product Purpose

Unchurnly is a revenue protection layer for indie Stripe businesses. It intercepts cancellations with retention offers (cancel flows), recovers failed payments with automated dunning sequences, and surfaces the outcome in a single dashboard. Success looks like: a founder sees money they would have lost, stayed. The product works quietly in the background and only asks for attention when it has something to show.

## Brand Personality

Calm. Precise. Protective.

Voice: measured, never alarmist. Speaks in specifics (dollar amounts, percentages, sequence counts) not abstractions. Treats the founder as competent — no hand-holding copy, no celebration of minor events. The interface is the reassurance.

## Anti-references

- **Intercom / HubSpot**: chatty, bubbly, rounded-everything SaaS aesthetic. Too friendly-casual for a tool that handles real revenue risk.
- **Baremetrics / ChartMogul**: metric overload, charts as the hero, analytics-first layout. Unchurnly is an action tool, not an analytics platform — the chart is context, not the point.
- **Linear / Vercel**: ultra-minimal dark developer aesthetic. Too cold and technical. Unchurnly serves founders, not engineers; it needs a human register within the dark palette.

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet authority** — The dashboard should reassure, not alarm. Numbers speak for themselves; the interface does not need to shout. Every element earns its presence or is removed.
2. **Revenue clarity over metric density** — Show what matters (money protected, active risk) without overwhelming a founder checking under stress. One clear story per screen, not a data room.
3. **Earned confidence** — The tool should feel like it has been running reliably in the background long before the founder opened it. Stable, unhurried, never reactive or trendy.
4. **Founder-paced** — Primary user is anxious and time-poor. Every interaction should reduce cognitive load. Defaults over choices; status summaries over raw tables; outcome over activity.
5. **Color carries meaning** — The dark theme is a deliberate control-room choice, not aesthetic fashion. Emerald means money saved. Amber means risk active. Red means loss. Never use these colors decoratively.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort, no formal WCAG compliance requirement. Minimum bar: all text legible at its rendered size on the dark background, keyboard navigable for primary flows, no motion that can't be suppressed. Contrast checked on every dark-surface text element before shipping.
